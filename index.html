Wedding Ceremony

Kijfhoek 30, 3335 LE Zwijndrecht.

The church stands on a ridge of the Devel, a former river arm. The oldest entry of the church dates back to 1388. It was probably enlarged around 1500 with the ship that was renovated in the 17th century and modified in the 19th century. In 1992/1993 the church was restored, costing about 1.7 million guilders. During the restoration, the piscine, a water basin, found in the south-east façade of the choir, has been restored.

This grittily urban building – a former power station – with an uncompromisingly bold and raw style is set out of the city centre but close to the river. It's relaxed and friendly, with an open-plan bar, café and deli, and a rooftop terrace for warmer days, and is also a cool, locals' hang-out.
